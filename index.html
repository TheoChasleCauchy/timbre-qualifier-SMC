Predicting Timbre Traits for Interpretable Assessment of Musical Sound Synthesizers

Théo Chasle Cauchy, Modan Tailleur, Mathieu Lagrange, Lindsay Reymore, Fanny Roche

Contact: theo.chasle-cauchy@ls2n.fr

Abstract

Quantitative assessment of the performance of neural audio synthesizers is now routinely performed using distribution based metrics such as the Fréchet Audio Distance (FAD). While this metric can be correlated with human perception, it can hardly be used to provide interpretable results, besides a mere ranking of different approaches. In this paper, we introduce a deep neural musical timbre trait predictor, with the shallow learnable part trained using the RWC musical instrument audio database and human judgments of 20 different timber traits (e.g. woody, percussive, rumbling, etc) for 31 different instruments. Satisfactorily, the predictor is found to have a good correlation with average human ratings (Pearson correlation of about 0.743 with p value of 9.6e-110). We then demonstrate the benefit of considering such predictor to assess the performance of TokenSynth, a sound synthesizer. First, interestingly, the MAE computed over the set of generated sounds provides the same ranking as a FAD computed with the RWC database as a reference, suggesting that the proposed predictors is able to provide equivalent information on a distributional basis. Second, by its ability to analyze sounds in isolation we are able to determine which generated sounds could be improved and qualify what needs to be improved.
