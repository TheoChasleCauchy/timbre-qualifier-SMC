Predicting Timbre Traits for Interpretable Assessment of Musical Sound Synthesizers

Théo Chasle Cauchy, Modan Tailleur, Mathieu Lagrange, Lindsay Reymore, Fanny Roche

Contact: theo.chasle-cauchy@ls2n.fr

Abstract

Automatic timbre qualification remains a challenging task in music information retrieval due to its inherently multi-dimensional and perceptual nature. While traditional signal processing methods and recent deep learning approaches have advanced the field, they often fail to establish a clear link between computed features and human perception. Reymore established timber traits profiles for orchestra instruments asking 243 musicians to rate 20 meaningful perceptual attributes. In this paper, we introduce a multi-layer perceptron model that predicts timber traits profiles using deep neural embeddings of instrument samples. We evaluate its performance and analyze errors, then demonstrate its utility by assessing the synthesis quality of the advanced sound synthesizer TokenSynth. Our results show promising accuracy in predicting perceptual attributes, with potential applications in improving the realism of synthesized sounds and aiding musicologists in instrument analysis. Our approach bridges the gap between computational models and perceptual timbre attributes, offering a novel framework for both research and practical applications in music technology.
